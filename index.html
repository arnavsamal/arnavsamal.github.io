I am an undergraduate student, majoring in computer science and engineering at the National Institute of Technology Rourkela, India, under the guidance of Prof. Tapas Kumar Mishra. I am broadly interested in multimodal deep learning and theoretical (probabilistic) machine learning. My current work focuses on leveraging large language models (LLMs) for enhancing mathematical reasoning, including tasks like proof generation, verification, and natural language explanation.

Previously, I had the privilege of spending a wonderful summer as a Research Intern at the Indian Institute of Technology, Hyderabad, India, under the guidance of Prof. Konda Reddy Mopuri. During this time, and in the subsequent months of remote collaboration, I conducted research on Explainability in Vision Transformers. My work involved developing post-hoc, model-agnostic techniques and exploring token pruning methods to enhance the interpretability of image classification tasks.

For more details, refer to my resume or drop me an email.
